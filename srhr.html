SRHR & Maternal Health

Empowering youth and women with rights, information, and access.

Introduction

Maternal health is a critical component of overall public health and a cornerstone of human development. Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR) encompass a broad range of services, information, and rights that are essential for the well-being of individuals and communities.

The Challenge: Barriers to Access

  • High Impact: High rates of teenage pregnancy, early marriage, and FGM.
  • Limited Services: Limited youth-friendly services and trained health personnel.
  • Cultural Taboos: Stigma around sexuality and reproductive health discussions.
  • Infrastructure: Inadequate infrastructure and long distances to health facilities.

Why VYBE Focuses on SRHR

Youth are disproportionately affected by poor SRHR outcomes. Inclusive governance must include health rights and bodily autonomy. Empowering young people leads to healthier communities. SRHR is a gateway to gender equality and social justice.

What We Do

Advocacy & Education

We train adolescent champions to lead peer education and advocacy, breaking the silence on taboo topics.

Community Dialogues

We facilitate intergenerational dialogues to challenge harmful cultural norms and foster understanding.

Strengthening Services

We partner with health facilities to improve youth-friendly services and ensure respectful care.

Accountability

We use community scorecards and social accountability tools to monitor service delivery and demand better health outcomes.
